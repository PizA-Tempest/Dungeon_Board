## Project Overview

Dungeon Board is a turn-based fantasy **board game RPG** inspired by **Dungeons & Dragons**, **Monopoly**, and party games.

Players move around a dungeon-themed board, roll dice, face monsters, trigger events, and grow stronger through **classes, races, items, and choices**.

The goal is not to recreate full D&D complexity, but to capture its **fantasy, chaos, and player-driven stories** in a short, replayable board-game experience.

This project follows a **vibe-first, MVP-driven approach**: fun before features, experience before systems.

## Game Fantasy

You are an adventurer trapped inside a living dungeon-board.

Each step forward is a roll of fate:

* monsters ambush you
* treasures empower you
* curses ruin your plans
* rivals sabotage your progress

Only one adventurer (or team) can dominate the dungeon and claim victory.

---

## Target Players

* Casual and mid-core players
* Friends playing together via **private rooms**
* Solo players who enjoy smart AI opponents
* Players who like:

  * D&D themes
  * board games
  * party chaos
  * replayable strategy

Players must **log in before playing**, creating a sense of identity, progress, and ownership.

Sessions should feel **short, punchy, and replayable**, not long or exhausting.

---

## Project Goal

Create a **fully playable MVP** of Dungeon Board that:

* requires **user login before playing**
* allows players to **create or join rooms**
* supports **1â€“4 players** per room
* supports playing with **friends or bots**
* uses a **board-based movement system**
* includes **8 classes** and **8 races** (mix & match)
* features combat, events, loot, and progression
* can be completed in a single play session

Success means:

* players feel connected through accounts and rooms
* solo play is still fun with bots
* the game is fun even with minimal content
* players understand how to play without tutorials
* each match tells a slightly different story

---

## Creative Principles

* Fun > realism
* Clarity > complexity
* Chaos is a feature, not a bug
* Systems must support player stories
* Every mechanic should create a decision or emotion

Avoid:

* over-engineering
* deep math-heavy RPG systems
* long setup or learning time
